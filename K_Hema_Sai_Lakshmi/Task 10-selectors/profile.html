John Doe

John Doe is a placeholder name used primarily in the United States and other English-speaking countries to represent an anonymous or unidentified male. The name is often used in legal contexts, medical scenarios, and various official documents when the true identity of the person is either unknown or needs to be kept confidential.

About Me

Chiefly in the US, an anonymous party, typically the plaintiff, in a legal action. Recorded from the 18th century, John Doe was originally in legal use as a name of a fictitious plaintiff, corresponding to Richard Roe, used to represent the defendant.
